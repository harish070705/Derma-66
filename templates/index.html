Introducing , a web-based clinical reference to identify skin lesions

Search

Our platform lets you search by picture. Just take a photo of a skin lesion, and our app will analyze it using advanced AI-driven algorithms against a proprietary image database of diagnosed lesions.

Designed for medical professionals, dermatologists, and patients, this tool provides a fast, reliable, and accurate way to assess skin conditions.

Early detection is crucial—our intelligent system helps differentiate between common skin conditions and potential concerns, guiding users towards appropriate medical advice.

About Us

This web-app helps you quickly analyze a skin lesion. It works in a similar way to a textbook, where you might find an image that looks like your skin problem. But while looking this up in a textbook would take a lot of time, this web-app does it in an instant!
Unlike traditional diagnosis methods that require manual examination and expert analysis, our application leverages deep learning models trained on a vast dataset to provide instant and accurate results.
Our mission is to create an accessible, cost-effective, and easy-to-use skin disease detection tool that can assist both individuals and healthcare professionals.
Our system's algorithm is unique among smartphone applications, using sophisticated CBIR (content based image recognition) technology to discriminate skin-lesion types.

How it Works

The Algorithm

The algorithm processes the uploaded image by enhancing clarity and extracting key features. A CNN-based model, trained on diagnosed skin conditions, classifies it into one of seven categories. CBIR technology compares the input with similar cases in the database. It then predicts the disease with confidence scores, ensuring accurate identification.

The Database

The database consists of 10,000 high-quality diagnosed skin disease images, covering seven distinct categories. By leveraging content-based image recognition (CBIR), the system analyzes uploaded images and matches them to the most relevant diagnosis.
